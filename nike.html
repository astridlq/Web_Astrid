Nike Digital Showroom Experience

CONTEXT

I’ve worked at Nike as Digital Go to Market (DGTM) Experience Specialist.
In this DGTM team, we aimed to improve the user experience for our B2B clients (Intersport, Footlocker, SDI…) through the 4 phases of the order process.

We supported Account Executives (AE) to:

  • Prepare: get the different offerings ready for their clients
  • Present: sell the offerings with story marketing content
  • Collaborate: fine tune orders together with the clients
  • Finalize: place orders through the Nike B2B online shop.

I had the opportunity to work on several project for different phases of the projects. For instance:

  • Present: Virtual Reality experience for the Football category
  • Finalize: Digital collection book for Intersport

After 1 year being on the field, dealing with AE and clients, I acquired a 360 understanding of this digital B2B experience.
That’s how I moved into another position to manage and develop the Virtual Showroom Experience for several accounts.

Design Process.

BACKGROUND RESEARCH


Ordering process & showroom setup.

Nike EMEA orders a huge number of samples for each new collection to distribute it across all the territories for the sell-in period (which occurs 4 times a year).
I assisted many times the AEs in the placement of their order. I also took care of setting up the showrooms with the correct samples, which took on average 3-4 days with 5 people working full-time.
I noticed that AEs were often very busy to prepare for their sales meetings and they had not a lot of time to help to setup the showrooms. This was a real pain point for them, even though it was crucial they helped since they were the ones with the knowledge of the products.
Besides many duplicates, samples were sometimes not even in their final version: some were missing, other ones are not in the correct colour/material.

Client meetings.

I assisted to different types of client meetings (from big accounts to small ones). Clients often followed PowerPoint presentations on a small TV from a distance taking notes on a printed version that was given to them at the beginning of the day. I realised that missing samples or wrong ones were a real struggle for AEs to lead their meeting efficiently.

PROBLEM

We gathered as a team to highlight the different problems after sharing our observations. We came up with some "How might we" questions to help us frame the main issue:

  • How can we reduce samples waste (production, shipment, storage, wrong samples)?
  • How can we change the showroom setup process to enable AEs to focus on the preparation of their meetings?
  • How can we make sure AEs have the correct samples to present during their sales meeting with clients?
  • How can we improve the B2B overall showroom experience to make it more interactive and efficient?

GOAL: To create a premium digital selling experience
in order to land the account offering.

JOB-TO-BE-DONE

As an Account Executive, when preparing for sales meeting, I want to be sure I have the correct materials to work, so I can lead interactive sessions with my clients efficiently.

IDEATION

We investigated on several solutions to answer this. To do that, used different technologies such as holograms, 3D modelling, or iBeacon…

The one that we deciced to move forward with was the Virtual Showroom Experience.
Some of the reasons were:

  • Technology: the easiest solution to implement within all the showrooms across Europe.
  • Costs: the most cost-effective solution we’ve found for the long term in terms of investments and of savings on the sample orders.
  • Time: a very flexible solution that can be adjusted last minute by the AE.


The idea was to create a virtual showroom which would showcase the 3D models of the collections presented during the sell-in sessions.
The PowerPoint presentations of the sell-in meetings were displayed on small TV fixed on the wall. This created friction when client groups were big. That's why we decided to use a large TV with touchscreen on wheels:

  • It was easily transportable within the showroom from one room to the other.
  • The touchscreen was necessary to virtually walk in that virtual space and to interact with products
